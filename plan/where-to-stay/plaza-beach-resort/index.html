Where to stay

Plaza Beach & Dive Resort

Van der Valk Plaza Beach & Dive Resort is the closest Bonaire comes to a full-service resort: beachfront in the Belnem corridor just south of Kralendijk, with three pools, a private marina, a PADI dive centre with its own house reef, the live-cooking restaurant Momento, and the island's one proper all-inclusive option. It's also home to the monthly Michelin Plaza Invites dinners and sells day passes if you're staying elsewhere. Below are the room types, grouped by category — each linking through to the resort's own website.

Note: the "from" prices are indicative starting rates; current details and photos are on the resort's own website. An all-inclusive upgrade is available on most room types.

Good to know

  • All-inclusive: Plaza is Bonaire's main all-inclusive address — the upgrade covers meals and drinks across the resort, including Momento's live-cooking dinners.
  • Diving on site: a PADI dive centre, house reef and tank station mean you can dive from your doorstep. See diving on Bonaire.
  • Getting around: it's a short hop from town, but you'll still want wheels to explore the island.
  • Nature fee: everyone entering the water needs the STINAPA Nature Tag, house reef included.

FAQ

Is Plaza Resort Bonaire all-inclusive?

It offers Bonaire's main all-inclusive option as an upgrade on most room types — a rarity on an island with essentially no all-inclusive scene. You can also book room-only.

Does Plaza Resort Bonaire have a dive centre?

Yes — a PADI dive centre with a house reef and tank station on site, so you can dive straight from the resort. Nature Tag required like everywhere.

Can non-guests use Plaza Resort Bonaire?

Yes, via a day pass (pools, beach, restaurant) — details on our beach clubs & day passes page. The monthly Plaza Invites Michelin dinners are also open to non-guests.
